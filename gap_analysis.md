# Gap Analysis: On-Chain Transaction Networks as Structural Causal Models for Systemic Risk in Crypto Markets

## TL;DR
- **The core gap is genuinely open.** As of June 2026, no published paper uses an on-chain blockchain transaction network (UTXO graph, exchange-flow network, DeFi token-transfer/wallet-to-wallet graph) as a Pearl-style structural causal model (SCM) — with directed causal edges and do-operator/counterfactual semantics — to measure systemic risk or contagion in cryptocurrency markets. The two most novel sub-claims (counterfactual VaR/CoVaR and on-chain-graph-as-SCM) are both unfilled.
- **The building blocks all exist separately.** Causal VaR via SCMs (Rigana/Wit/Cook's Causal-NECO VaR), directed-information causal systemic-risk networks including crypto (Etesami/Habibnia/Kiyavash), correlation-network CoVaR contagion (Giudici/Parisi CoRisk), on-chain transaction-graph risk modeling (Akcora et al.), and DeFi systemic-risk/contagion analysis (BIS, Lehar-Parlour, ASRI) are each well developed — but none combines on-chain graph structure + Pearl SCM + a systemic risk measure.
- **Recommendation: proceed, but position precisely.** Frame the contribution as the first to (a) treat the on-chain transaction graph as the structural causal model rather than a correlation/Granger network, and optionally (b) extend to a counterfactual (Pearl Level-3) systemic risk measure. Cite Causal-NECO VaR as the nearest methodological precedent and the crypto network-CoVaR literature as the nearest application precedent.

## Key Findings

1. **No counterfactual VaR/CoVaR exists.** The exact terms "counterfactual CoVaR" and "counterfactual value at risk" return no finance hits. The contagion-based VaR literature is explicitly correlational: as Rigana, Wit & Cook (arXiv:2402.06032) put it, methods "such as CoVaR (Tobias and Brunnermeier, 2016), SDSVaR... focus on correlations in systemic risk rather than causality." (The Adrian–Brunnermeier NBER working paper w17454 likewise describes ΔCoVaR as capturing an institution's marginal contribution "in a non-causal sense.") Pearl's Level-3 (counterfactual) layer has not been applied to VaR or CoVaR.

2. **No on-chain-transaction-graph-as-SCM exists.** Existing crypto systemic-risk networks are built from price-return correlations or Granger/transfer-entropy causality, not from on-chain flows treated as a structural causal graph. Papers that do use on-chain graphs (Akcora et al.; stablecoin-contagion tracing) use them predictively or observationally, explicitly disclaiming causal-structural interpretation.

3. **The closest methodological precedent is Causal-NECO VaR** (Rigana, Wit & Cook, 2024), which computes VaR from an SCM/causal contagion network discovered via the PC-stable algorithm — but operates at Pearl Levels 1-2 only, on Forex data, with no counterfactual layer and no on-chain/crypto data.

4. **The closest crypto application precedent is the directed-information systemic-risk framework** of Etesami, Habibnia & Kiyavash (2023), which builds time-varying directed causal networks across financial sectors including cryptocurrencies — but uses directed information (a generalized Granger causality), not a Pearl SCM, and uses returns, not on-chain data.

## Details

### Angles 1 & 2: Causal VaR/CoVaR landscape

**Causal-NECO VaR — Rigana, Wit & Cook (2024), "Navigating Market Turbulence: Insights from Causal Network Contagion Value at Risk," arXiv:2402.06032.** Authors at Swiss Finance Institute/USI Switzerland and FNA UK. Method: decomposes asset log-returns into an autoregressive part and an instantaneous "contagion" part via a structural equation model; discovers the causal structure with the PC-stable algorithm of Colombo and Maathuis (2014) — "a more robust version of the original PC algorithm (Spirtes and Glymour, 1991)," implemented via the R package pcalg — then estimates contagion coefficients by least squares and computes a Gaussian or Gaussian-copula VaR. Data: simulation studies plus 20 exchange rates over the USD, January 2000 to April 2021, as published by the Federal Reserve of New York (each series 5,101 observations, no missing values). Contribution: VaR that is robust/invariant to market shocks because it rests on directional causal contagion, not correlation. Limitations: Levels 1-2 only (no counterfactuals); Forex not crypto; assumes no unmeasured confounders — the authors note that "in cases involving unmeasured confounders and latent variables, the Fast Causal Inference Algorithm (FCI)... presents a suitable alternative" as future work; not on-chain.

**Foundational lineage:** Rigana, Wit & Cook (2023), "A new way of measuring effects of financial crisis on contagion in currency markets," International Review of Financial Analysis 90:102764 — combines causal networks with a structural VAR (extending Giudici & Parisi 2018), explicitly using Pearl (2009) causality concepts via Spirtes-Glymour and Colombo-Maathuis, noting prior VAR approaches "look at" correlation, not causal direction.

**Etesami, Habibnia & Kiyavash (2023), "Modeling Systemic Risk: A Time-Varying Nonparametric Causal Inference Framework," arXiv:2312.16707.** Introduces the Time-Varying Directed Information Graph (TV-DIG), an information-theoretic generalization of Granger causality, nonparametric and able to capture nonlinear, non-pairwise, time-varying interconnections. Data: 124 assets — 113 stocks across GICS Banks (4010), Diversified Financials (4020), Insurance (4030), REITs (6010), plus a KFTX-based Fintech group, and 11 cryptocurrencies that "together account for approximately 75% of total crypto market capitalization" — spanning January 1, 2018 to January 1, 2023. The paper bills itself as "the inaugural study that investigates the evolving dynamics of systemic risk posed by crypto assets to financial networks," detecting heightened crypto-to-sector influence during COVID-19 and around "the collapse of Terra in May 2022, which erased $50 billion in valuation, and the FTX crisis." Limitation: directed information ≠ Pearl SCM; returns not on-chain; no VaR/CoVaR measure or counterfactual.

**Giudici & Parisi CoRisk:** "CoRisk: Measuring Systemic Risk Through Default Probability Contagion" (2016, SSRN 2786486) and "CoRisk: Credit Risk Contagion with Correlation Network Models," Risks 6(3):95 (2018). Combine partial-correlation networks with VAR to derive conditional default probabilities and decompose systemic risk into idiosyncratic + contagion components. Limitation: correlation networks, not causal SCM.

**Standard CoVaR:** Adrian & Brunnermeier, "CoVaR," American Economic Review 106(7):1705-1741 (2016) — the canonical conditional systemic-risk measure, described in the precursor NBER working paper as non-causal.

**Extreme-risk SCM (traditional finance, not crypto):** Klüppelberg & Krali (2025/2026), "Causal analysis of extreme risk in a network of industry portfolios," arXiv:2504.00523, published in the Canadian Journal of Statistics 54:e70041 (doi:10.1002/cjs.70041). Uses a max-linear structural equation model — each node a max-linear function of its DAG parents plus exogenous innovation — to identify directed paths of extreme-risk propagation, with structure learning via hard-thresholding. Applied to 30 industry equity portfolios; confirmed to contain no crypto/on-chain content. Related lineage: Gissibl & Klüppelberg (2018) recursive max-linear models; Améndola, Klüppelberg, Lauritzen & Tran (2022, Ann. Appl. Probab. 32(1):1-45).

### Angle 3: Counterfactual risk measures

No "counterfactual VaR" or "counterfactual CoVaR" exists. Pearl's counterfactual (Level-3) layer is widely used in ML fairness and explanation, and counterfactual scenario analysis appears in systemic-risk policy work — e.g., "Identifying systemic risk drivers in financial networks" (Physica A, 2018) holds fixed all risk drivers but one over evolving BIS cross-country networks; NBER w25405 evaluates counterfactual network topologies. But these are scenario/simulation counterfactuals on exposure networks, not Pearl Level-3 abduction-action-prediction applied to a tail risk measure. A credit-portfolio "counterfactual analysis" paper (Xu 2025, Financial Engineering and Risk Management 8:6-13) uses the word but not as an SCM-based risk measure. Broader surveys (e.g., "A Comprehensive Review of Causal Inference in Banking, Finance, and Insurance," ACM Computing Surveys 2025, doi:10.1145/3736752) document only association/Granger usage around SRISK/MES/CoVaR — no counterfactual CoVaR.

### Angle 4: Blockchain/on-chain as causal graph

On-chain graphs are used extensively, but predictively or for forensics, not as SCMs:
- Akcora, Dixon, Gel & Kantarcioglu, "Bitcoin Risk Modeling with Blockchain Graphs," arXiv:1805.04698 / Economics Letters (2018) — "chainlets" (subgraphs) predict Bitcoin price/volatility; Granger-predictive, not SCM/contagion.
- "Tracing Stablecoin Contagion during the USDC Depeg after the Silicon Valley Bank Collapse," arXiv:2606.07442 (2026) — high-granularity on-chain transaction data reconstruct contagion pathways from market-wide synchronization down to account-level reallocation; observational/associational, explicitly not causal-structural.
- Fraud/AML graph work (RiskSEA/Coinbase, arXiv:2410.02160; GAT-ResNet on the Elliptic dataset; UTXO clustering) — risk scoring of addresses, unrelated to systemic risk measures.
- "SoK: Decentralized Finance (DeFi) Attacks," arXiv:2208.13035 — applies structural equation modeling to DeFi incident causality, but on incident attributes (with latent variables), not transaction graphs, and not systemic risk.
- "Systemic Risk by Design? Causal Evidence on Endogenous Blockchain Security" (MIT Sloan working paper, 2025/26) — uses on-chain Ethereum data with instrumental-variables causal inference on Proof-of-Work security (e.g., a 10% fee increase yielding ~12.5% rise in likely-strategic settlement reversals), not a transaction-graph SCM and not a contagion/systemic-risk-measure focus.

### Angle 5: Online/sequential causal systemic risk

No real-time/sequential causal systemic-risk algorithm for crypto exists. Adjacent: Sadeghi, Gopal & Fesanghary, "Causal Discovery in Financial Markets: A Framework for Nonstationary Time-Series Data" (CD-NOTS), arXiv:2312.17375 / International Journal of Data Science and Analytics (2024, doi:10.1007/s41060-024-00679-7) — handles nonstationary, lagged + contemporaneous causality but is batch, on traditional assets. The TV-DIG framework (Etesami et al.) is rolling-window — the nearest to "adaptive" — but not an online updating algorithm and not on-chain. Scalability of time-series causal discovery is a recognized bottleneck (e.g., VarLiNGAM speedups, Mathematics 13(20):3288, MDPI 2025).

### Angle 6: DeFi-specific systemic risk

A rich and rapidly growing literature, none combining on-chain data with Pearl causal inference:
- Lehar & Parlour, "Systemic fragility in decentralized markets," BIS Working Paper 1062 — liquidation contagion across the crypto ecosystem, balance-sheet style.
- "Systemic Risk in DeFi: A Network-Based Fragility Analysis of TVL Dynamics," arXiv:2601.08540 (2026) — correlation-network fragility of Total Value Locked.
- DeXposure-FM, arXiv:2602.03981 (2026) — graph foundation model over 43.7M observations spanning 4,300+ protocols, 602 blockchains and 24,300+ tokens for inter-protocol credit exposure; graph ML, not SCM.
- ASRI, Farzulla & Maksakov, arXiv:2602.03874 (2026) — Aggregated Systemic Risk Index from four weighted sub-indices (Stablecoin Concentration 30%, DeFi Liquidity 25%, Contagion 25%, Regulatory Opacity 20%) using DeFi Llama, FRED and on-chain analytics; validated on Terra/Luna, Celsius/3AC, FTX, SVB; explicitly states it captures DeFi-specific vulnerabilities "that SRISK and CoVaR cannot accommodate." Composite index, not a causal SCM.
- "Mapping Systemic Tail Risk in Crypto Markets: DeFi, Stablecoins, and Infrastructure Tokens," JRFM 18(6):329 (2025) — partial-correlation tail network, explicitly undirected and model-free.
- "Contagion in Decentralized Lending Protocols: A Case Study of Compound" (DeFi'23, HAL-04221228) — balance-sheet contagion network on Compound.

## Recommendations

1. **Proceed — the gap is real and defensible.** Frame the novelty explicitly as: "the first to use the on-chain blockchain transaction network as the structural causal model for a systemic risk measure in crypto markets," and (if you build it) "the first counterfactual systemic risk measure." Both sub-claims were independently confirmed as unfilled across standard term combinations.
2. **Position against the two nearest neighbors.** Differentiate from Causal-NECO VaR (you: on-chain graph as SCM + crypto + counterfactual; them: return-based SCM, Forex, Levels 1-2) and from TV-DIG / CoVaR-Granger crypto work (you: Pearl SCM on on-chain flows; them: directed information / correlation on returns). Both should be cited in your contribution statement as the closest prior art.
3. **Anticipate the confounding/identification critique — this is your biggest reviewer risk.** The PC/PC-stable and directed-information literatures assume no unmeasured confounders; reviewers will ask whether on-chain edges are genuinely causal rather than mechanical flow accounting. Pre-empt with explicit identification assumptions, an FCI-type robustness check (as Causal-NECO's own authors flag), or an instrument (e.g., protocol-level exogenous shocks, in the spirit of the MIT Sloan endogenous-security IV design). Be precise that you use a genuine Pearl SCM, not Granger causality or directed information — that distinction is what makes the gap defensible but also raises the bar.
4. **Benchmark rigorously.** Backtest your causal/counterfactual measure against ΔCoVaR, MES/SRISK, Diebold-Yilmaz connectedness, and ASRI on the canonical crypto stress events: Terra/Luna (May 2022), Celsius/3AC (June 2022), FTX (Nov 2022), and the USDC depeg / SVB collapse (March 2023). Standard VaR backtests (Kupiec unconditional/conditional coverage, dynamic quantile) should accompany the systemic-risk validation.
5. **Thresholds that would change the recommendation:** if a 2025-2026 working paper surfaces defining "counterfactual CoVaR," or using a token-transfer/UTXO graph as a DAG/SCM for contagion, narrow the claim to the specific online or counterfactual increment rather than abandoning the project. Given the velocity of 2026 crypto-systemic-risk preprints, re-run the targeted searches immediately before submission.

## Caveats
- Negative existence claims are bounded by search coverage (web/arXiv/Semantic Scholar as of June 2026); very recent or un-indexed working papers using different terminology could exist. Both gap claims were cross-checked by an independent search pass and held up, but treat them as "no evidence of," not absolute proof of absence.
- The DeFi/crypto systemic-risk literature is moving very fast — several of the most relevant items (ASRI, DeXposure-FM, USDC-depeg tracing, the DeFi-TVL fragility paper) are 2026 preprints not yet peer-reviewed — so the gap could narrow before publication. Verify immediately pre-submission and treat preprint claims as provisional.
- "Causal" is used loosely across this literature: Granger causality, directed information, and Pearl SCMs are mathematically distinct. Your contribution's novelty rests on deploying a genuine Pearl-style SCM (with do-operator/counterfactual semantics) on on-chain data — which sharpens the gap but concentrates the methodological burden on credible causal identification.
- The Journal of Risk Finance audience leans applied/managerial; ensure the SCM machinery is matched by clear practitioner-facing interpretation (e.g., which wallets/protocols "export" vs. "import" systemic risk, and what a counterfactual intervention would imply for regulators), since the nearest precedents (Causal-NECO, TV-DIG) target more econometric venues.
